Han's philosophical theory of globalized culture, organized around the concept of *hyperculture* — the condition that results when globalization does not merely connect sites but *dissolves the site as such*, detaching cultural forms from their historical and spatial embeddedness. The book is an early work (2005), lighter in argument than his later books but establishing several concepts he carries forward throughout his career.

## The Concept of Hyperculture

Globalization produces a *side by side* — a simultaneity of heterogeneous cultural forms in a borderless space. The correct term for this condition, Han argues, is not inter-, multi-, or trans-cultural but *hyper*cultural. Interculturality presupposes essentialist cultures in dialogue. Transculturality implies crossing borders; hyperculture has no borders to cross. Multiculturality manages "tolerance" between fixed differences. The *hyper*, by contrast, names accumulation, compression, and de-distancing: cultures don't encounter each other — they implode into a single hyperspace organized not by the OR (selection between) but by the AND (simultaneous availability of everything).

The hypermarket, the hypertext, Xanadu (both Coleridge's pleasure-dome and Ted Nelson's hypertext system): these are Han's recurring figures for the hypercultural space. The web is a "seascape" but not Hegel's frightening ocean of philosophical uncertainty — it is a tourist sea, comfortable and de-distanced. *Logos* gives way to *hyperlogue*.

## De-Siting and De-Auratization

The site (*Ort*) in the strong sense is where Being has auratic depth — Benjamin's "unique apparition of a distance," tied to a specific *here and now* that cannot be repeated *there*. Globalization *de-sites* culture: forms detach from their historical and ritual contexts and enter the hypercultural simultaneity. This dissolves the aura.

But Han refuses the standard lament. [[De-Auratization]] is not simply loss; it is "another shape of Being," a *hyperreality* with its own splendour. Hearst's castle near San Simeon — a bricolage of cultural goods from all ages and styles, forgeries seamlessly combined with genuine articles — is offered as a miniature museum of hyperreality, an anticipation of the global condition.

[[De-Siting]] is also *de-facticization*: cultures detached from their spatial-historical *thrownness* (Heidegger) are open to free appropriation. This increases freedom — the *homo liber*, who chooses from the hypermarket of cultural practices, is the figure of hypercultural existence.

## Pilgrims, Wanderers, and Tourists

Three figures structure the book's phenomenology of movement:

**The pilgrim** (Bauman's modern figure): walks across a desert-like earth toward a *There*, driven by the painful asymmetry of Here/There. Modernity, pilgrimage, and Heidegger's "on the way to" share this structure. The pilgrim's world must be "orderly, determined, predictable" — footprints engraved for good.

**Nietzsche's wanderer**: moves across a de-teleologized world without a final destination, capable of *hypervision* (seeing what is really going on) precisely because no ultimate meaning claims his gaze. Still melancholic, still a pilgrim of sorts — his nights are bad, the desert abuts the town gate.

**The hypercultural tourist**: knows no asymmetry between Here and There. *There* is only another *Here*. Fully inhabits the space of immanence. Neither longing nor fear. The here is struckthrough: He͟r͟e͟ — de-auratized, no longer a site in the strong sense, but not a loss. Culture becomes *cul-tour*.

Han's coup de grâce: Heidegger, the philosopher of the site, the house, and the homeland, is himself a *tourist*. His collection of "things" (jug, ploughshare, heron, cross — monosyllabic, alliterative, artificially simple) is a romantic counter-world *projected* onto peasant life, not genuine rootedness. His "threshold" — which "petrifies pain" — is the philosophical figure of *homo dolores*, the suffering native, set against the tourist's serene smile.

## Windows and Monads

Leibniz's windowless monads (closed substances, guaranteed by pre-established divine harmony) versus Nelson's hypertextual *windowing* (the step-through-the-window as mode of experience). Windowing is the hypercultural mode of being-in-the-world: open, networked, de-internalized, with no isolated subjects. But the window also seals: *windowing* can produce new monads, isolated before screens, who may need a new God.

## Hypercultural Identity and the Rhizome

Identity under hyperculture is patchwork — assembled from the hypermarket of cultural practices according to aesthetic inclination rather than inheritance. Han calls this the *coloured Self* (vs. the monochrome self of cultural essentialism). Time becomes "naked": de-narrativized, point-like (Flusser's "universe of bits"), without the suspense of a story. This is painful loss and new freedom simultaneously.

The best structural model for hyperculture is Deleuze and Guattari's **rhizome** — non-centred, non-dialectical, alliance rather than filiation, structured by the AND rather than the either/or. The rhizome displaces Bhabha's hybrid interstitial space (too dialectical, still organized by colonial antagonism) and Heidegger's bridge (too theological, gathering before the divinities). Hyperculture is a *hyphen-culture* — the hyphen connects without demanding a deeper inner connection, like fungal hyphae (*hyphé*: woven, net, web) forming a mesh with no centre.

## Key Concepts

- **[[Hyperculture]]**: The cultural condition produced by globalization — a side by side simultaneity of de-sited forms in a hyperspace organized by the AND-logic. Distinct from inter-, multi-, and trans-culturality.
- **[[De-Siting]]**: The dissolution of the *site* as the anchor of cultural identity and Being. The primary operation of globalization, producing hyperculture.
- **[[De-Auratization]]**: The decay of Benjamin's aura (the "unique apparition of a distance") when cultural forms become de-sited and infinitely reproducible. Not simply loss — hyperreality as another shape of Being.
- **[[Homo Liber]]**: The free human of hypercultural existence — de-facticized Dasein, no longer thrown into an inheritance, navigating the hypermarket of possibilities.
