Han's figure for the form of human existence enabled by [[Hyperculture|hyperculture]] and [[De-Siting|de-siting]]. The *homo liber* is de-facticized Dasein: released from *thrownness* (Heidegger's term for finding oneself always already thrown into a particular inheritance — site, language, tradition, body), free to project existence into the hyperspace of possibilities rather than being constrained to "the possibility it has inherited."

The hypercultural tourist is the incarnation of *homo liber*: already at home in the hyperspace of attraction, they are equally at home everywhere. They do not seek a final destination. They move from one Here to another Here. Microsoft's "Where do you want to go today?" encodes this de-facticized existence — the question presupposes a subject who has no given destination, only options.

## Ambivalence

Han does not straightforwardly celebrate *homo liber*. The freedom is real — the tourist's serene smile against Heidegger's *homo dolores* petrified into a threshold of pain. But this freedom also hollows out:
- **Time** becomes "naked" (Flusser) — point-like, without narrative horizon, without the suspense of story
- **Identity** becomes patchwork — the *coloured Self* assembled from the hypermarket, which may be vitality or may be the fragmentation of what was once a meaningful whole
- **Windowing** (Nelson's figure for hypercultural being-in-the-world) may produce new monads: isolated before screens, open to everything, held by nothing — who may, after all, need a new God

The question Han leaves open: is the tourist's happiness — the de-facticized, serene, site-free happiness of *homo liber* — more or less desirable than the bounded happiness Herder describes, which depends on a deafness to other voices?
