Han's term for the cultural condition produced by globalization. Hyperculture is not what happens when cultures meet, dialogue, or cross borders — those are the models of inter-, multi-, and trans-culturality, which all presuppose essentialist cultures with stable identities. Hyperculture names what happens when globalization dissolves the *site* as such: [[De-Siting|de-siting]] detaches cultural forms from their historical and spatial embeddedness and places them in a borderless side by side, a simultaneity of everything simultaneously available.

## The AND-logic

The organizing principle of hyperculture is the AND, not the OR. Where cultural essentialism selects and excludes (you are either this culture or that one), hyperculture accumulates, compresses, and de-distances. Heterogeneous contents — from different sites, ages, traditions — are pushed together without hierarchy or organizing telos. This is what the "hyper-" marks: not a superlative of culture but its *implosion* into a hyperspace.

Han's figures: the hypermarket, the hypertext (Nelson's "Xanadu"), the web as seascape. The surfer is not Hegel's philosopher thrown into an ocean of uncertainty — they are a tourist in a comfortable, familiar sea. *Logos* gives way to *hyperlogue*: not a continuation of dialogue but the departure from the order of the old logos altogether.

## Hyperculture vs. related concepts

- **Interculturality**: presupposes dialogue between essentialist cultures; rooted in Western nationalism and colonialism
- **Multiculturality**: manages tolerance between fixed differences; little space for mutual penetration
- **Transculturality** (Welsch): emphasizes crossing borders; hyperculture has no borders to cross — there is no hiking, only browsing
- **Hybridity** (Bhabha): still too dialectical, organized by colonial antagonism; hyperculture is friendlier, more rhizomatic than Bhabha's interstitial stairwell
- **Rhizome** (Deleuze/Guattari): Han's preferred structural model — non-centred, non-dialectical, the AND of "and … and … and …"

## Hyphen-culture

Han's etymological move in *Hyperculture*: the hyphen (*hyphé* in Greek: woven, net, web) connects without demanding inner connection. Hyphae — fungal filaments — form a mesh with no centre, no proper root. Hyperculture is a *hyphen-culture*: it hyphenates rather than roots, allies rather than filiates. The rhizomatic mesh of culture spreads without a core.

## The hypercultural tourist

The primary figure of hypercultural existence: see [[De-Siting]] for the contrast between pilgrim, wanderer, and hypercultural tourist. The tourist inhabits the space of Being-Here without asymmetry, without pain, without the dialectics of Here and There. [[Homo Liber]] is the philosophical name for this form of existence — de-facticized Dasein navigating the hypermarket of possibilities.
